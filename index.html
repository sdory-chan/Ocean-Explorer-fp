Pioneers of the Deep: Humanity’s First Steps into the Abyss!
In 1960, oceanographer Jacques Piccard and U.S. Navy lieutenant Don Walsh descended into the Mariana Trench, reaching a depth of 10,915 meters aboard the bathyscaphe Trieste. 
Surrounded by absolute darkness and crushing pressure they witnessed unexpected life—a flatfish swimming in the abyss, proving that even the deepest ocean harbors living creatures.
